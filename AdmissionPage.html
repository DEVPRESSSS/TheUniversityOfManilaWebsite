Admission

High School and Elementary Department

Admission to the University of Manila is a privilege and not a right. As a general rule, The University of Manila is open only to students whose qualifications indicate satisfactory academic performance and who could meet other admission requirements as heretofore required. In the interest of maintaining academic standards and preserving peace and tranquility in the campus, the University reserves the right to refuse enrollment to any applicant.

Admission Requirements for Elementary and High School Department

Old Students should present their last year’s Final Report Card (F-138) or a certificate from their principal to the effect that they passed all the subjects in the previous year.

New Students for the Elementary (except Grade 1) and High School courses should present a duly accomplished transfer or final report card or high school transcript of records.

Admission Policies

Credentials submitted for enrollment become permanent portions of the school records and may not be withdrawn after registration.

Any applicant who is not able to present the credentials required within registration period will be automatically classified as a special student—one who is not entitled to receive college credits for work done during the entire school year, semester, or summer term.

No student is allowed to enroll in The University of Manila and in another educational institution during the same semester or term under penalty of losing credits in one or in both institutions, unless previously approved by the Commission on Higher Education, and a written permit for the same is recorded in the office of the University Registrar. The permit should state the subjects taken in the other school.

No student may enroll in an advanced subject or subjects, under penalty of losing credits in the same, before he has completed the corresponding prerequisite subject or subjects in the preceding academic year or years. One should therefore enroll only in subjects duly approved by the respective heads of the department concerned.

Students are allowed to attend only the classes for which they are duly registered and validly enrolled.

A student wishing to register for courses in a college, school, or department of the University of Manila other than in which he has already enrolled is required to secure prior approval in writing from the Deans of the colleges, schools, or departments concerned.

Changes of the courses or subjects, colleges, etc., may be made only upon written approval of the Dean concerned and the University Registrar but in no case after the registration period. Copies of said request for changes of subjects or courses or dropping of same, together with the written approval of the Dean or Heads concerned, should be filed with the Office of the Registrar and the Accounting Division. Changes are effected on the date applications therefore are filed with said offices.

No credits will be given for: (a) Subjects taken by students for which they did not register as shown by their approved application for enrollment; (b) Subjects taken by students who are not fully qualified to take the same; (c) Subjects not duly approved by the deans or department heads concerned; (d) Subjects taken by special students.

Payments of school fees is required upon registration and is requisite to valid enrollment.

In all cases, students should consult the Deans or Department Heads concerned in preparing their program of study.

Conduct and Discipline

The University of Manila expects that a student seeking admission into any of its colleges comes with an honest purpose. For this reason, it is demanded that each student uphold not only the recognized standards of the University pertaining to morality, good conduct, and courtesy, but also the regard of his fellow students and the faculty, and the dignity of the University.

Character

The University of Manila, in order to maintain a high moral standard among its student population invokes the following:

  • The University reserves the right to refuse admission:
    • Of any student found to have been charged in Courts of Justice with any crime involving moral turpitude;
    • Of students who are members of subversive organizations;
    • Of student-transferees who obtained Failures or Incomplete grades in 50% of the academic load of the preceding semester.
  • This refusal may apply even to students already admitted and enrolled in the University.
  • In case of doubt as to character fitness of an applicant/enrollee, the University authorities have the right to demand from the applicant, clearance from the Police, or other government entities concerned.
  • Failure of the student to present required credentials for admission and other supporting documents within the prescribed period will be sufficient cause to invalidate his admission to the University.
  • The University may discharge any student for immorality, drunkenness or any gross misconduct against fellow students, faculty members, or administrative officials enumerated in Sec. IX, Par. 217 of the MANUAL OF INFORMATION FOR PRIVATE SCHOOLS.

College Admission Policies and Guidelines

Admission to the University of Manila is a privilege and not a right. As a general rule, the University is open only to students whose qualifications indicate satisfactory academic performance and who can meet the required admission standards. The University reserves the right to refuse enrollment to any applicant to preserve academic integrity and campus peace.

1.1 Entrance Examinations

Policy Guidelines and Conditions

  • Students must qualify for admission through entrance exams and meet all other requirements.
  • Exams are administered by the Admission Office and approved by the Registrar’s Office.
  • Applicants must submit grades from previous academic work.
  • PEPT holders must obtain clearance from the Registrar’s Office.
  • Applicants must complete the exam application and pay fees.
  • If not qualified for their first course choice, applicants may be considered for other courses based on cutoff scores.
  • Extraordinary cases require approval from the President and Chief Academic Officer.

1.2 Admission

The University accepts students who meet academic standards set by the Committee on Admission and who agree to follow University rules.

Admission Policy

  • Entrance exam results (passing does not guarantee program admission).
  • Performance in interviews with officials or admission heads.
  • Recommendations from previous school officials.

Admission Requirements

  • Original Form 138 (Report Card)
  • Certificate of Good Moral Character from school principal/guidance counselor
  • Written undertaking signed by student or guardian to abide by university rules
  • Credentials must be verified; falsification leads to disqualification

1.3 Enrollment

Policy Guidelines and Conditions

  • Enrollment is only valid after official registration and payment of fees.
  • Forms are released at the Accounting Office and pre-numbered for control.
  • Enrollment must occur during designated periods only.
  • Prerequisites must be met for subject credit to apply.
  • Overloads are only allowed in the last two semesters before graduation or in special cases.
  • Old students must present grade clearance and ID.
  • Proxies for enrollment are discouraged.
  • Only enrolled students may attend classes and receive class cards.
  • ROTC is optional for male students.
  • Students enroll through their respective college officials; scholars enroll through their committee reps.

1.3.1 Transferees

  • Transfer applications are limited to exceptional cases.
  • Entrance exam is required with enrolling official’s clearance.
  • No failing grade in subjects credited in the program applied for.
  • Falsified records or previous disciplinary actions disqualify the applicant.
  • Transfer credentials and new student enrollment approval required.

1.3.3 Cross Enrollment

  • Cross-enrollment permitted only for valid reasons (e.g., scheduling conflicts, courses not offered).
  • Approval from the Dean and Registrar required.
  • Only subjects listed on the permit may be taken.
  • Student must provide certification of grades after term ends.
  • Cross-enrollees from other institutions must present permits and follow official schedules.

1.3.4 Summer Class Enrollment

  • Summer classes are intended for back subjects; advanced subjects are generally not allowed.
  • Regular load is 9 units; graduating students may take 12 units under conditions.
  • Absences: Max 6 hours for a 3-unit class. Tardiness (2x) = 1 absence.
  • Summer classes run for 31 days, Monday to Saturday.
  • All regular enrollment policies still apply.

1.4 Academic Load

  • Students must follow regular program load guidelines.
  • Graduating students may request up to 6 units of overload.
  • Students may not pursue multiple degrees at the same time.
  • Graduating students may be allowed 8 units of PE if it's their final requirement.

Conduct and Discipline

The University expects all students to uphold standards of morality, courtesy, and respect for faculty, fellow students, and the institution itself.

Character

  • The University reserves the right to refuse admission to:
    • Applicants charged with crimes involving moral turpitude;
    • Members of subversive organizations;
    • Transferees who failed 50% or more of their previous academic load.
  • These policies may apply even after a student has already been admitted.
  • If the applicant’s character is in doubt, police or official clearance may be required.
  • Failure to submit credentials within the required period invalidates admission.
  • Students may be expelled for immorality, drunkenness, or gross misconduct.

Graduate Studies Admission Requirements

Department of Education Excerpt

    • Proficiency in required foreign language

  • Residence: Minimum of 3 semesters, or equivalent summer terms. Completion required within 5 years.

Admission Policies

  • Incomplete credentials lead to "special student" status with no credits earned.
  • Simultaneous enrollment in another school is not allowed unless permitted by CHED.
  • Advance subjects require completion of prerequisites.
  • Students must register and attend only approved courses.
  • Changes in enrollment require written approval and Registrar recording.
  • Credits will not be given for improperly registered subjects or subjects taken as a special student.
  • Payment of school fees is required at registration.

Conduct and Discipline

The University expects students to uphold morality, good conduct, and respect toward peers and staff.

Character Requirements

  • Refusal of admission for students charged with crimes involving moral turpitude.
  • Refusal of students linked to subversive organizations.
  • Refusal of transferees with 50% failing/incomplete grades.
  • Police clearance may be required for character concerns.
  • Failure to provide credentials may invalidate admission.
  • Dismissal may occur for immorality, intoxication, or misconduct.

College of Law: Admission Policies and Requirements

Legal Basis

Admission into the Law course is governed by Section 6, Rule 138 of the Rules of Court of the Philippines, which requires completion of a bachelor’s degree in arts or sciences with a major in Political Science, Logic, English, Spanish, History, or Economics from a recognized institution.

This is further supported by CHED Circular No. 46, series of 1996, which grants Higher Education Institutions (HEIs) the responsibility to determine student eligibility provided the following minimum requirements are met:

  • Must be a bachelor’s degree holder
  • Earned at least 18 units of English
  • 6 units of Mathematics
  • 18 units of Social Science subjects

UM College of Law Admission

Admission is determined by the Dean based on compliance with legal requirements and performance in a written exam and oral interview. The College of Law admits qualified students regardless of age, sex, religion, or educational background.

New Student Requirements

Applicants must take the Law Entrance Examination (LEX), administered by the Dean’s office. Exams are conducted from March to June (1st Sem) and October to early November (2nd Sem).

Requirements:

  • Original or certified photocopy of Transcript of Records
  • Certificate of Good Moral Character
  • NSO Birth Certificate
  • Certificate of Transfer Credentials (Honorable Dismissal)
  • Three (3) copies of recent 2x2 photo

Transfer Students

Transfer admissions are reviewed case-by-case by the Dean. Decisions are based on academic credentials and qualifications.

Requirements:

  • Honorable Dismissal from previous school
  • Certificate of Grades
  • Certificate of Good Moral Character
  • NSO Birth Certificate
  • Certificate of Eligibility for Law Admission (Form C-1), if available
  • Three (3) copies of recent 2x2 photo

Cross-Enrollment Policy

Cross-enrollment is allowed under meritorious cases determined by the Dean:

  • If a subject is not offered by the College
  • If subject schedule conflicts with other classes

Advance Standing

Granted to students who have completed 50% or less of the required units from another institution. Special cases are referred to the Dean and/or CHED for approval.

Special Students

Students may enroll under professors with prior approval. They will:

  • Sign a waiver agreeing to no final exams
  • Follow regular academic and disciplinary rules
  • Pay the standard registration fee plus per-course fees

Note: Courses taken as special students are not validated for credit even if entrance requirements are later fulfilled.

International Students

International applicants are subject to an initial interview conducted by the University President and Chief Academic Officer. This ensures alignment with institutional and academic expectations.

Requirements:

  • Submission of academic records
  • Presentation of original passport
  • Submission of a photocopy of the passport to the Registrar’s Office
  • Payment of application, testing, and other corresponding fees

Scholarships and Grants

ACADEMIC SCHOLARSHIP

New Student

Valedictorians are granted free tuition fees for a period of one academic year (2 semesters). Salutatorians are granted free tuition fees for a period of one-half academic year (one semester). Both of which may be extended, provided that they meet the conditions set by the Scholarship Committee.

Term Scholarship

For College Student – One and one-half scholarship are granted every semester to students who obtain the two highest averages under the following conditions:

College:

  • For Full Scholarship: 1 to 1.5 (94-99)
  • For Partial Scholarship: 1.51 to 1.75 (91 to 93)

Averages will be computed on the following bases:

  • Academic Average: 50%
  • Social Consciousness and Extra Curricular Activities: 25%
  • Character: 25%

Criteria:

The academic rating will be based on the weighted average of at least 1.75 of the student’s grades, the minimum load depending upon the curriculum of each college for the semester immediately preceding the award.

The social consciousness and character ratings of the prospective scholars will be determined by the pooled appraisal of all faculty members under whom the students took the course during the term in question.

In case of a tie, the same will be broken by splitting the award. In case more than one student qualifies within the first bracket (1- 1.5) the first two highest will be entitled to the full and a half-semester scholarships, respectively.

The University, however, reserves the right to cancel all privileges granted to any holder of a scholarship in case the recipient fails to maintain the required proficiency in the University or live up to the accepted rules of good behavior.

OTHER SCHOLARSHIPS

Athletics Scholarship

This scholarship is particularly extended to basketball players, who are granted free tuition fees with allowance and free board and lodging accommodations.

Caritas Scholarship

Financially challenged but academically deserving students who are recommended by CARITAS are given 100% discounts on tuition fees provided they meet the conditions set by the Scholarship Committee.

Career Orientation Scholarship

Dr. Emily D. De Leon Scholarship Grant

Offered to financially-challenged but academically deserving students, who graduated from the current year and a graduate of Manila public schools, with an average grade of 85% and above, and should be able to maintain an average as provided by the Scholarship Committee.

Scholarship for Faculty Members Taking up their Graduate and Post Graduate Studies

Faculty members who are undertaking their graduate and/or postgraduate studies are given 100% free tuition fees.

Madame Emiko Yamamoto Scholarship Grant

A full scholarship grant given to students with very high grades and should be able to maintain a high average grade.

NSC – DECS Scholarship (SYDP, PESFA)

Grantees are sponsored by the National Scholarship Center of the Department of Education, Culture and Sports after qualifying for admission in the University.

Philippine Veterans Administration Grants

Grantees are sponsored by the Philippine Veteran Administration (PVA) after qualifying for admission in the University. A certification from PVA is required to be presented to the Admission and Accounting Offices.

Subsidy Law P.D. 451

This scholarship is granted by the University of Manila to deserving and qualified students in compliance with the ‘Subsidy Law’ or Presidential Decree 451.

University Band Members

Members of the University Band are granted free tuition fee with allowance and are exempted from P.E. and ROTC.

DISCOUNTS

Faculty / Employees’ Dependents

100% discounts from their tuition fees.

Multiple Sibling Discounts

Three or more brothers and/or sisters enrolled in the University of Manila granted discounts. One of them is given a 50% discount from the tuition fee and also the succeeding brother or sister.

Special Talents Discounts

A special discount of about 20% to 50% is given to students with special talents to be determined by the Scholarship Committee.

University Choir Members

30% discounts from their tuition fees.

Military and Police Discounts

Officers and Enlisted Men of the Armed Forces of the Philippines and the Philippine National Police are given 30% discount from their tuition fees.

INCENTIVES

The University of Manila gives cash incentives to graduate students who made it to top 10 of their respective government board examinations, adding to it is a rebate of their Board Review Fee.

University Registrar

The Office of the University Registrar plans, organizes, coordinates, directs, and controls activities involving student registration, transfer, graduation, and the maintenance and safekeeping of the student records.

It likewise provides a periodic evaluation of student academic performance in accordance with the requirements of the University and the Commission on Higher Education. The Admissions Office, Scholarships Section, Records 1, and Records 2 assist the Registrar’s Office.
